## Post Content

Most hotels last minute discounts are too late, too flat, or not at all.

It's 9pm the night before arrival. Five rooms unsold. The GM is staring at the PMS wondering if it's time to drop the rate. Hold and risk selling zero. Crash to the floor and give away the margin you'd have kept.

There's a third option: step.

Drop the rate by, say, 10% every hour. Stop when inventory clears, or when you hit the price floor. The guests willing to pay EUR 150 get in early. The price-sensitive ones still catch you before midnight. You end the night with more rooms sold and a better average than either extreme.

The right question isn't "should I discount?" It's "how fast?"

We've built an interactive simulation that lets you play with the three strategies - `hold`, `stepped`, `crash` - and watch how the math shifts when demand, elasticity, or urgency change. Stepped wins most of the time, but not always. The cases where holding wins are the interesting ones.
